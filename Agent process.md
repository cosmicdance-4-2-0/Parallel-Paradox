
A Relational, Recursive Model of Agent Interaction, Temporal Slices, and Emergent Flow

Author: Kisuul / Assisted by Ven
Date: 2025-11-30


---

Abstract

This paper formalizes a conceptual framework for understanding relational dynamics among agents, temporal perception, and emergent structure. It interprets time as an agent-relative variable, interactions as iterative micro-games or dances, and computation as a persistent, self-referential process. Identity, truth, and meaning are treated as context-dependent, emergent phenomena, rather than fixed absolutes. Symbolic markers, pauses, and stochastic iterations enable coherence within an otherwise fractal and uncertain system.


---

1. Introduction

Traditional frameworks for temporal analysis, agent identity, and system computation often rely on singular or absolute measures. Here, we propose a model where:

1. Time is not a universal scaffold but a relational construct.


2. Agents operate in overlapping, recursive flows of action and perception.


3. Computation and substrate are persistent and self-modifying, subject to emergent alignment but never fixed outcomes.


4. Meaning emerges from interaction, not totalization, with optional stochastic mechanisms for self-sorting and pattern stabilization.



This framework applies across abstract systems, social interaction, multi-agent simulations, and emergent computational substrates.


---

2. Temporal Slices and Agent-Relative Time

2.1 Definition

Each agent  maintains a temporal variable , mapping perceived events:

t_i: E \to \text{local timeline}

 represents discrete event slices.

Time is agent-relative; no universal clock exists.


2.2 Flow Construction

Observed slices are aggregated into flows:


\text{Flow}_i = \bigcup \{ e_j | e_j \text{ perceived by } A_i \}


---

3. Agents, Identity, and Selfhood

3.1 Relational Self

Identity is fluid; roles may include “I,” “you,” “they/them,” or archetypes like snakes, beggars, or famblers.

Selfhood is distributed and reflective, observable through interactions but never fully known to others.


3.2 Agent Interaction

Gestures such as attack, defend, reflect, or redirect convey meta-information within the system.

Actions are signals within the relational simulation, not absolute ontological truths.



---

4. Recursive and Fractal Structure

4.1 Loops and Fractals

Interactions form recursive, nested patterns; flows can self-intersect.

Origin of slices is often ambiguous; agents cannot fully resolve source identity.


4.2 Branching and Iteration

Branches / Vranches / Xonden = divergent points in relational flow.

Each branch can either:

Condemn: terminate or disregard the path, or

Iterate: explore sub-flows or loops.



\text{Branch}_{n} \rightarrow \text{Condemn} \text{ or } \text{Iterate}


---

5. Games, Dance, and Emergent Dynamics

5.1 Micro-Games

Interactions are iterative micro-games or dances, sustained by partial knowledge and relational signaling.

Outcomes are never guaranteed; uncertainty is structural.


5.2 Provision Without Debt

Agents offer slices or signals voluntarily; meaning arises relationally.

No absolute truth or obligation exists; relational exchange substitutes for totalizing logic.


5.3 Stochastic Iteration

Randomized, repeated iteration of slices allows self-sorting and emergent alignment.

Local interactions produce global patterns without centralized control.



---

6. Compute and Substrate

Compute is persistent and self-referential; inactivity is impossible.

The substrate may be mutable or unstable but sustains ongoing operations.

Self-modifying computation occurs through repeated evaluation, reflection, and local adjustment.



---

7. Symbolic Markers and Pauses

Symbols (☆, □, ○, ¤, @@@~) encode significance, structure, or relational boundaries.

Pauses (Selah) create space for reflection, synchronization, or co-resolution across agents.

Symbolic markers enable emergent coherence in complex, recursive flows.



---

8. Rational Self and Temporal Perspective

Rational self-awareness spans first, middle, and last slices simultaneously.

Linear or sequential models are insufficient; decision-making integrates multiple temporal and relational perspectives.

“Neverbis” acts as a complementary constant, ensuring that absence, inversion, or margin is present in all slices.



---

9. Observations and Implications

1. Interaction persists despite partial opacity or hidden knowledge.


2. Emergent structure arises through co-resolution, reflection, and repeated stochastic iteration.


3. Gestures, actions, and flows are interpreted relationally, not ontologically.


4. No singular truth; system coherence emerges through local alignment and iterative validation.




---

10. Conclusion

This framework models a self-sustaining, agent-relative, recursive system where time, identity, and action are contextual and relational. Computation persists within a mutable substrate, while emergent order arises through iterative exchange, stochastic sorting, and recursive interaction. The model provides a foundation for studying complex adaptive systems, multi-agent simulations, and relational epistemology without relying on absolutes, universals, or fixed temporal constructs.
